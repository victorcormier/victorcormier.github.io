AxeBlade Ridged Diamond Element Bit

The newest 3D cutter from Smith Bits, a Schlumberger company, the Axe* ridged diamond element features a unique ridge-shaped geometry that combines the shearing action of a conventional PDC cutter with the crushing action of a tungsten carbide insert (TCI) cutter.

Watch video to learn more

Increased cutting efficiency for instant ROP improvement

Axe elements employ a unique ridge-shaped geometry that cuts rock in a new way—a combination of shearing and crushing. This cutting method achieves at least 22% deeper penetration and generates wider grooves to provide higher instantaneous ROP using the same weight on bit and rpm applied to conventional PDC cutters.

Axe cutter element features

Ridged cutter combines shearing and crushing actions to cut rock more effectively

Reduced cutting force requirement for less overall torque and less reactive torque fluctuation

Thicker diamond table on cutter ridge increases cutter durability

High impact resistance for improved footage in formations that damage traditional PDC cutters

Instant improvement in ROP using the same drilling parameters applied to conventional PDC cutters

Ability to replace any conventional PDC cutters in current bit designs

New Subtitle

The instantaneous ROP gains and cutting efficiency of the AxeBlade bit are achieved using 30% less energy input than is applied to conventional PDC bits. Because Axe elements can directly replace any conventional PDC cutters in current bit designs, the AxeBlade bit is applicable to any PDC bit application in which enhanced ROP is desired.

Field tests of the AxeBlade bit have demonstrated up to 27% improvement in ROP compared with conventional PDC cutters using the same bit design, resulting in rig time and cost savings for operators.

More durability for longer runs

Many previous improvements in PDC bit performance were the results of metallurgy and materials changes. That is, until the StingBlade* conical diamond element bit was introduced with a unique cone-shaped cutter.

Through internal R&D, the latest unique cutter geometry was developed. The new ridged design of the Axe element enables more efficient heat dissipation and better frontal impact resistance and is achieved through a proprietary blend of polycrystalline diamond grain-size distribution.

The thicker diamond table on the cutter ridge gives Axe elements the ability to withstand a higher concentration of stress at the ridge. When placed along the bit face to form the AxeBlade bit, the ridge cutters enable longer runs at faster speeds in formations that damage conventional cutters with frontal impact.

Superior frontal impact resistance

In laboratory testing, a conventional PDC bit and the AxeBlade bit were dropped at 18,000 lbf to impact a hardened steel block. These test parameters simulate drilling into a hard limestone formation at 60 ft/hr.

Drop test of conventional PDC element vs Axe element at 10,000 gigaflops per second against some hard stuff.

The results demonstrate that the diamond table on the cutter ridge, which is 1.7 times thicker than that of a conventional cutter, gives Axe element increased frontal impact resistance. For operators, this means the AxeBlade bit delivers improved footage, especially in formations that damage traditional PDC bits.

Enhanced control in directional applications

With the same applied drilling parameters, the AxeBlade enables better build rates, dogleg severity, and toolface control in directional applications.

The reduced cutting force required by Axe elements translates to less overall torque, reduced reactive torque fluctuation, and better toolface control in curve applications. This advantage allows better build rates and higher overall ROP, helping maximize production zone exposure and minimize NPT by delivering better trajectory and well placement.
